# What I do

I work with engineering and product organisations that are working hard but not working well.

Most teams don’t struggle because they lack ideas, effort, or capability.
They struggle because the system is working against them — usually without anyone intending it.

I help identify what is actually getting in the way — whether that’s structure, decision-making, flow, or how the organisation has evolved — and then help fix it.

In most cases, the underlying issue becomes clear quite quickly once you look at how the system actually behaves.

## Key perspective

The organisations I work with aren't broken.

They've usually evolved — through growth, acquisition, changing priorities, or accumulated decisions — into a state where the structure is working against the work.

Systems tend to produce the outcomes they are set up for — even if that wasn’t the intention.

The useful question is not "what’s wrong?", but "what in the system is causing this, and what needs to change to shift it?"

The goal isn't "transformation", it's targeted, practical change that removes constraint and lets the team breathe.

## Where I typically get involved

* Delivery feels slower than it should be
* Teams are busy but progress is inconsistent
* Work requires too many people / teams to complete
* Decisions are slow, escalated, or unclear
* AI and tools are increasing output, but not outcomes
* Leadership senses something is off, but can’t clearly articulate why

## How this is different

There’s no shortage of advice on how engineering teams should work.

The problem is that much of it breaks down under real-world conditions.

I'm not an analyst who studies these problems from the outside.

I’ve spent 25+ years inside engineering organisations, responsible for making delivery work in practice — under pressure, with constraints, and with imperfect systems.

That experience changes the approach:

* Focus on what actually happens, not what should happen
* Identify the real constraint, not a list of issues
* Make fewer, higher-impact changes
* Work with the system as it exists, not an idealised version

## How I work

Engagements are short, focused, and designed to get to clarity quickly.

### 1. Diagnostic (1–2 weeks)

A short, focused engagement to understand how the system actually works in practice.

* Targeted interviews across engineering and product
* Identification of flow, friction, structure, and decision dynamics
* Clear articulation of what is happening and why

Output:

* Clear problem definition
* Identification of the primary constraint
* Practical, prioritised actions

### 2. Synthesis (2–3 days)

Translate findings into a clear, shared understanding of what’s actually going on.

* What’s happening
* Why it’s happening
* What is actually limiting progress
* What to change (and in what order)

### 3. Intervention (optional, 2–6 weeks)

Support the organisation in removing or reducing the constraint.

* Clarifying ownership and decision-making
* Reducing unnecessary complexity or coordination
* Improving flow and focus
* Introducing practical changes that stick
